Help on: Check covariables

Why should I check covariables?
It is necessary to check the covariables for two reasons: to make sure the data has been correctly read by Pomelo II and to check if the added information is not to related to class (e.g. classTumor are all males and classNoTumor are all females).

Have they been correctly read?
Covariables can be of two types, either numeric or non-numeric. If the data does not have the correct format, you may find the numeric data has been read as non-numeric or vice versa.

Are any of the additional covariables "invalid"?
In general if the values of a certain covariable are grouped in clearly separated groups and group in the same way as the class labels (eg. All subjects belonging to class A are under 20 whereas class B subjects are all over 20) this data will probably make Pomelo II crash. Warning: If you find this case, the fact that you do not use this covariable does not stop your analysis from being incomplete, in the sense that genes that appear as differentially expressed may be due to that covariable and not to class difference.

What do the graphs show?
The graphs of the numeric covariables, show first the probability density distribution and next to it a box plot showing the positions of min, max, 1st Quart, mean and 3rd Quart of each class.
Whereas the non-numeric covariable graph shows the total number of counts for each covariable value and the number of counts per class.
